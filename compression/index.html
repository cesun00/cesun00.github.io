The DEFLATE data format and Gzip archive format

June 16, 2024
DEFLATE DEFLATE is a data format used to store and transmit compressed data. Specifically, it’s an abuse to refer to DEFLATE as a compression algorithm. The DEFLATE data format was designed Phil Katz, for version 2 of his PKZIP archiving tool, and later standardized as the RFC 1951. The most popular and de-facto standard DEFLATE library is the zlib. Overview A DEFLATE formatted file consists of a sequence of blocks. Each block is comprised of 2 parts: a description of a Huffman tree, followed by the actual compressed data.
